## Partnership Models to Consider

### 1. Affiliate Partnership
- They promote your tool, earn 20-30% commission
- Best for: Influencers with engaged audiences
- Pros: Performance-based, low risk
- Cons: Requires tracking, may not prioritize

### 2. Co-Branded Content
- Create guide/video/course together
- Best for: Content creators (YouTubers, podcasters)
- Pros: Authentic, adds value
- Cons: Time investment

### 3. Sponsorship
- Pay to sponsor podcast, event, or content
- Best for: Established shows/events
- Pros: Guaranteed exposure
- Cons: Expensive, may not convert

### 4. White Label
- They resell your tool under their brand
- Best for: Coaches with training programs
- Pros: Recurring revenue, loyalty
- Cons: Less brand recognition

### 5. Referral Fee
- Flat fee for each customer referred
- Best for: Brokers, team leaders
- Pros: Simple, scalable
- Cons: Requires volume

### 6. Free for Their Students
- Give free access to their coaching students
- Best for: Coaches with programs
- Pros: Adds value to their offer, user acquisition
- Cons: No immediate revenue

## Outreach Strategy

### Phase 1: Build Relationship (Weeks 1-4)
1. Follow and engage with their content daily
2. Comment thoughtfully, share their posts
3. Join their communities (FB groups, newsletters)
4. Attend their free events
5. Tag them in relevant content (don't be annoying)

### Phase 2: Warm Outreach (Weeks 5-8)
1. Send DM/email with genuine compliment
2. Share something valuable (research, insight)
3. Ask a thoughtful question
4. Build rapport over multiple touches

### Phase 3: The Ask (Weeks 9-12)
1. Propose specific partnership idea
2. Make it about their audience, not you
3. Offer value first (free access, content, revenue share)
4. Be clear about what you're asking

### Sample DM Sequence:

**Message 1 (Engagement):**
"Hey [Name], loved your latest video about [topic]. The point about [specific thing] really resonated—exactly what I'm hearing from agents I'm interviewing."

**Message 2 (Value Add, 1-2 weeks later):**
"Hey [Name], I just finished interviewing 30 agents about their biggest CRM frustrations. Happy to share the full report if it'd be helpful for your content. No strings, just thought you'd find it interesting."

**Message 3 (The Ask, after relationship built):**
"Hey [Name], I've been following your work for a while and love how you help agents with [topic]. I built a tool that solves [specific problem you talk about]. Would you be open to trying it out and giving feedback? If you think it'd help your audience, I'd love to explore a partnership."
