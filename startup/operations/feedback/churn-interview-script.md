# Churn Interview Script

**Purpose:** Conduct exit interviews with customers who cancel to understand why they left and identify patterns for improvement

**Goal:** Reduce churn by learning from departed customers and addressing root causes

---

## 1. Interview Overview

### When to Conduct Interviews

**Priority Customers (Always Interview):**
- ✅ Team Plan customers ($199+/month)
- ✅ Customers with 6+ months tenure
- ✅ High-usage customers (> 10 transactions)
- ✅ Strategic accounts (referrers, influencers, case studies)

**Standard Customers (Sample Interview):**
- Interview 20-30% of churned Professional/Starter customers
- Select randomly or based on interesting patterns

**New Customers (Interview All):**
- ✅ Customers who cancel within first 30 days
- Understand onboarding failures

---

### Interview Timing

**Best Time:** Within 7 days of cancellation (memories are fresh)

**Method:**
- Phone call (preferred - 15-20 minutes)
- Video call (optional, if customer prefers)
- Email survey (fallback if they won't take call)

---

### Incentives

**Offer:**
- $50 Amazon gift card, OR
- 1 month free if they return, OR
- Donation to charity of their choice

**Why:** Increases response rate, shows you value their time

## 2. Outreach Email

### Initial Outreach

**Subject:** Quick question about your experience (+$50 gift card)

**Body:**

```
Hi [Name],

I noticed you recently canceled your [Product Name] subscription. I'm sorry to see you go, but I want to understand what happened.

Would you be willing to share 15 minutes of your time for a quick phone call? I'd love to learn:
- What worked well for you
- What didn't work
- What we could do better

**In exchange:** I'll send you a $50 Amazon gift card as a thank you for your time.

**Your feedback matters:** It helps us improve for future customers.

👉 [Book a time that works for you]

If you'd prefer not to talk, I'd still value your feedback via this 2-minute survey: [Link]

Thank you for giving [Product Name] a try.

[Signature]
[Phone number - shows you're real person]
```

---

### Follow-Up (If No Response)

**Send:** 3 days later

**Subject:** Re: Quick question about your experience

**Body:**

```
Hi [Name],

Just following up on my previous email. I know you're busy, but I'd really value your feedback.

If you have 15 minutes, I'd love to chat: [Book a time]

Or, if easier, just reply to this email with:
1. What was the #1 reason you canceled?
2. What would have made you stay?

Either way, thank you for your time.

[Signature]
```

---

### Final Attempt (If Still No Response)

**Send:** 1 week after first email

**Subject:** Last request - we want to learn from you

**Body:**

```
Hi [Name],

One last request - I promise!

If you have 30 seconds, just reply with one thing: What's the #1 reason you canceled?

That's it. Even a one-word answer helps.

Thanks for your time, and best of luck!

[Signature]
```

## 3. Interview Script (Phone/Video)

### Opening (2 minutes)

**You:** "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from [Product Name]. Thanks so much for taking the time to chat with me today. I really appreciate it.

Before we start, I want you to know:
1. There are no wrong answers - I want honest feedback
2. This isn't a sales call - I'm not trying to win you back
3. This will take about 15-20 minutes
4. Is it okay if I record this for internal notes? (Won't be shared externally)

Sound good?"

---

### Background Questions (3 minutes)

**You:** "Great. Let's start with some background to help me understand your situation better."

1. **"How long were you using [Product Name]?"**
   - [Note: < 30 days, 1-3 months, 3-6 months, 6+ months]

2. **"What plan were you on?"**
   - [Starter / Professional / Team]

3. **"How many transactions were you managing in [Product Name]?"**
   - [Number]

4. **"What was your role?"**
   - [Solo agent / Team lead / Transaction coordinator / Other]

---

### The Decision to Cancel (5 minutes)

**You:** "Now I'd like to understand what led to your decision to cancel."

5. **"What was the PRIMARY reason you decided to cancel?"**
   - [Let them talk - don't interrupt]
   - [Probe: "Tell me more about that..."]

6. **"Were there other factors that contributed to your decision?"**
   - [Note all reasons]

7. **"When did you first start thinking about canceling?"**
   - [Recently / A while ago / Before you even signed up]

8. **"Did something specific trigger the decision, or was it a gradual realization?"**

---

### Product Experience (5 minutes)

**You:** "Now I'd like to ask about your experience using the product itself."

9. **"What worked well for you? What did you like about [Product Name]?"**
   - [Note positives - these are what to keep]

10. **"What didn't work well? What was frustrating or disappointing?"**
    - [Note pain points - these are what to fix]

11. **"Was there a specific feature or capability you expected that we didn't have?"**
    - [Note missing features]

12. **"How was the onboarding process? Did you feel like you understood how to use the product?"**
    - [If they mention confusion, probe deeper]

13. **"Did you contact our support team? If so, how was that experience?"**
    - [If yes, probe: What went well? What didn't?]

---

### Value & Alternatives (3 minutes)

**You:** "Let's talk about value and alternatives."

14. **"Did you feel like [Product Name] was worth the price you were paying?"**
    - [Yes / No / Unsure]
    - [Probe: Why or why not?]

15. **"What are you using now instead of [Product Name]?"**
    - [Competitor name / Manual system / Nothing / Other]

16. **"How does your current solution compare to [Product Name]?"**
    - [Better / Worse / About the same]
    - [Probe: In what ways?]

17. **"What would have made you stay? What could we have done differently?"**
    - [This is gold - listen carefully]

---

### Closing (2 minutes)

**You:** "We're almost done. Just a couple more questions."

18. **"Would you recommend [Product Name] to a friend or colleague? Why or why not?"**
    - [NPS-style question]

19. **"Is there anything else you want to share that I didn't ask about?"**
    - [Open floor to them]

20. **"If we made significant improvements based on your feedback, would you consider giving us another try in the future?"**
    - [Yes / No / Maybe]
    - [If yes: "What specifically would need to change?"]

---

### Thank You

**You:** "Thank you so much for your time and honesty today. This feedback is incredibly valuable, and I'm going to share it with our product team.

I mentioned a $50 Amazon gift card as a thank you - can you confirm the email address I should send it to? [Confirm email]

One last thing: If you ever decide to give us another try, email me directly at [your email] and I'll personally set you up with [incentive: extended free trial, discount, etc.].

Thanks again, [Name]. Best of luck with everything!"

## 5. Churn Reason Taxonomy

### Categories

**Product-Related:**
1. **Missing Features:** Needed functionality that doesn't exist
2. **Bugs/Reliability:** Product was broken or unreliable
3. **UX/Usability:** Too confusing, hard to use
4. **Performance:** Too slow, didn't work well

**Value-Related:**
5. **Price:** Too expensive, didn't see ROI
6. **Low Usage:** Didn't use it enough to justify cost
7. **Better Alternative:** Found something better

**Business-Related:**
8. **Business Change:** Closed business, retired, changed roles
9. **Team Change:** Left team, team dissolved
10. **Seasonality:** Slow season, will return later

**Support-Related:**
11. **Poor Support:** Bad experience with customer service
12. **Onboarding Failure:** Couldn't figure out how to use it

**External Factors:**
13. **Mandatory Tool:** Brokerage mandated different tool
14. **Integration Issues:** Didn't integrate with existing workflow

## 7. Acting on Churn Insights

### Common Actions

**If churn due to Missing Features:**
- Add to feature request backlog
- Prioritize based on frequency
- Communicate roadmap to prevent future churn

**If churn due to Price:**
- Consider lower-tier plan
- Better communicate value
- Add more value to justify price

**If churn due to UX/Usability:**
- Improve onboarding
- Simplify interface
- Add tooltips/guidance

**If churn due to Bugs/Reliability:**
- Prioritize bug fixes
- Communicate improvements
- Offer credit/discount for affected customers

**If churn due to Poor Support:**
- Train support team
- Improve response times
- Follow up with affected customers

**If churn due to Onboarding Failure:**
- Improve onboarding flow
- Add more tutorials
- Offer concierge onboarding

## 9. Churn Interview Metrics

### Track These

1. **Interview Response Rate:**
   - Target: > 50% of churned customers
   - If low, improve incentive or outreach timing

2. **Interview Completion Rate:**
   - Target: > 90% (people who say yes should complete)

3. **Time to Interview:**
   - Target: < 7 days after cancellation

4. **Insights Generated:**
   - Number of actionable insights per month
   - Features/fixes driven by churn interviews

5. **Win-Back Success Rate:**
   - % of churned customers who return
   - Target: > 10%

---

## 10. Churn Interview Best Practices

✅ **DO:**
- Make them feel heard, not sold to
- Ask open-ended questions
- Listen more than you talk
- Take detailed notes
- Thank them genuinely
- Follow up if you implement their feedback

❌ **DON'T:**
- Get defensive
- Argue with their experience
- Try to win them back during the interview
- Make promises you can't keep
- Rush the interview
- Skip the incentive

## Example: Completed Churn Interview

```markdown
# Churn Interview - Sarah Martinez

**Date:** March 15, 2026
**Interviewer:** Alex Johnson (CS Lead)
**Customer Email:** sarah@example.com
**Plan Tier:** Professional ($99/month)
**Tenure:** 4 months
**Transactions Created:** 8

---

## Primary Reason for Churn
Sarah canceled because she didn't feel the AI extraction was accurate enough for her contracts, which are often non-standard (commercial real estate). She found herself manually correcting data, which defeated the purpose.

## Secondary Reasons
- Price felt high given the extraction issues
- Missing feature: Commercial contract templates
- Wanted calendar integration (not available on Professional plan)

## What Worked Well
- Loved the client email templates - "saved me tons of time"
- Timeline visualization was helpful
- Document hub kept things organized

## What Didn't Work
- AI extraction struggled with commercial contracts (4/8 transactions had errors)
- Had to manually re-enter data too often
- No Google Calendar integration on Professional plan

## Missing Features
- Commercial real estate contract support
- Google Calendar integration on Professional plan
- Bulk document upload

## Value Perception
- Did they feel it was worth the price? **No**
- Why: "If the AI extraction worked for my contracts, yes. But since I had to manually fix half of them, it felt like I was paying for a feature I couldn't use."

## Alternative Solution
- What are they using now? **Dotloop**
- How does it compare? "Worse AI (none), but more reliable for standard workflows. I'm using manual entry in Dotloop now."

## What Would Have Made Them Stay
"Better AI extraction for commercial contracts, or at least acknowledgement that it's optimized for residential. Also, Google Calendar integration would have been huge."

## NPS (Would They Recommend?)
**Maybe** - "For residential agents, yes. For commercial, no."

## Would They Return?
**Maybe** - "If you improve commercial contract extraction and add calendar integration to Professional plan, I'd consider it."

## Key Quotes
> "The AI felt like it was built for residential, not commercial. That's fine, just tell me that upfront."

> "I loved the client email templates. That alone saved me 2-3 hours per week."

> "At $99/month, I expected the AI to work for my contracts. If it can't, I'd rather pay $50 for a simpler tool."

## Action Items for Team
- [ ] Add "best for residential" messaging to marketing/site
- [ ] Evaluate commercial contract extraction accuracy
- [ ] Consider Google Calendar for Professional plan
- [ ] Email Sarah when improvements launch

## Interviewer Notes
Sarah was very specific and constructive. Not angry, just disappointed. She clearly saw the value but the product wasn't optimized for her use case. I suspect there are other commercial agents with similar issues - worth investigating.
```
