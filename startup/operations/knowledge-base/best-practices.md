## Category 1: Getting Started Right

### Best Practice 1.1: Complete the Onboarding Checklist

**Why:** Users who complete onboarding are 3x more likely to become power users

**How:**
1. Don't skip the checklist (it takes < 10 minutes)
2. Upload a real contract (not sample) for best learning
3. Explore each feature thoroughly
4. Customize settings before heavy use

**Pro tip:** Do the onboarding during a live transaction - you'll learn faster with real stakes.

---

### Best Practice 1.2: Set Up Your Profile First

**Before uploading contracts, configure:**

1. **Time zone:** Ensures reminders arrive at right time
2. **Email preferences:** Daily digest vs. individual reminders
3. **Email templates:** Add your branding/signature
4. **Notification schedule:** 7/3/1 days vs. custom

**Why:** Settings affect all future transactions - configure once, benefit always.

---

### Best Practice 1.3: Use Descriptive Transaction Nicknames

**Bad:** "Transaction 1", "Contract", "Smith"

**Good:** "123 Main St - Smith Purchase", "456 Oak Ave - Jones Listing"

**Why:**
- Easier to find later
- Clear at a glance
- Helpful when you have 10+ transactions

**How:** When creating transaction, enter nickname in the field provided

## Category 2: Contract Management

### Best Practice 2.1: Upload Contracts Immediately

**When:** Within 24 hours of contract acceptance

**Why:**
- Sets up reminders immediately
- Reduces mental load
- No scrambling later

**Workflow:**
1. Contract accepted
2. Upload to [Product Name] (30 seconds)
3. Review extraction (1 minute)
4. Done - rest is automated

---

### Best Practice 2.2: Always Review AI Extraction

**Even if confidence is high (green), do a quick review:**

**Check:**
- ✅ Closing date correct?
- ✅ Contingency dates accurate?
- ✅ Parties spelled correctly?
- ✅ Property address right?

**Why:**
- 2% error rate means 1 in 50 might be wrong
- Catching errors early prevents bigger problems
- Takes 30 seconds, saves hours

---

### Best Practice 2.3: Add Custom Events Proactively

**Beyond contract dates, add:**

- Final walkthrough (2 days before closing)
- Client moving date
- Home warranty expiration
- Any important client-specific dates

**How:**
1. Transaction → Timeline → "Add Custom Event"
2. Enter details
3. Get reminders just like contract dates

**Why:** Never miss non-contract dates that matter

---

### Best Practice 2.4: Organize Documents with Tags

**Use tags for:**
- `urgent` - Needs immediate attention
- `buyer-signed` - Documents signed by buyer
- `seller-needed` - Waiting for seller signature
- `contingency` - Related to contingencies

**How:**
1. Upload document
2. Click document → "Edit Tags"
3. Add descriptive tags

**Why:** Find documents fast when you need them

## Category 3: Deadline Management

### Best Practice 3.1: Check Daily Digest Every Morning

**The daily email is your mission control:**

**When:** First thing in the morning (with coffee ☕)

**What to do:**
1. Scan all upcoming deadlines
2. Note any urgent items (red/orange)
3. Plan your day around deadlines
4. Take action on urgent items

**Time:** 30 seconds

**Impact:** Never surprised by deadlines

---

### Best Practice 3.2: Mark Tasks Complete Immediately

**Don't wait - mark complete right after finishing:**

**Why:**
- Updates timeline immediately
- Stops unnecessary reminders
- Keeps records accurate

**Example:** Inspection complete → Mark "Inspection Contingency" as complete → Add note: "Passed, no issues"

---

### Best Practice 3.3: Set Aggressive Reminder Schedules

**For high-stakes deadlines (e.g., financing contingency):**

**Customize reminders:**
- 10 days out
- 5 days out
- 3 days out
- 1 day out
- Day of

**Why:** More reminders = less likely to miss

**How:** Settings → Notifications → Custom schedule

---

### Best Practice 3.4: Use Timeline as Client Update Tool

**When clients ask "What's next?":**

1. Open timeline
2. Share screen or screenshot
3. Walk through upcoming dates

**Why:**
- Clients love visual aids
- Shows you're organized
- Builds trust

## Category 4: Client Communication

### Best Practice 4.1: Send Proactive Updates

**Don't wait for clients to ask - send updates at every milestone:**

**Minimum updates:**
1. Contract accepted
2. Inspection scheduled
3. Inspection complete
4. Appraisal complete
5. Clear to close
6. Closing complete

**Why:**
- Reduces client anxiety
- Fewer "checking in" calls/emails
- Builds trust and confidence

---

### Best Practice 4.2: Customize AI Emails

**AI drafts are 80% there - add the final 20%:**

**Add:**
- Personal details ("Hope the kids are excited about the backyard!")
- Local context ("Market is hot, glad we got this under contract")
- Your personality

**How:**
1. Select stage → AI generates email
2. Edit before sending
3. Add 1-2 personal sentences
4. Send

**Time:** 30 extra seconds
**Impact:** Feels more authentic, less robotic

---

### Best Practice 4.3: Set Expectations in First Email

**First client email should include:**

- When they'll hear from you next
- What to expect in the process
- How to reach you with questions

**Example template:**

```
Hi [Client Name],

[Standard AI content about contract acceptance]

**What's next:** Over the next few days, I'll be scheduling the inspection. 
You'll hear from me by [date] with options.

**Questions?** Reply to this email or text me at [your cell] anytime.

Looking forward to getting you into your new home!

[Signature]
```

---

### Best Practice 4.4: Respond to Client Replies Quickly

**If client replies to your update:**

**Target:** < 4 hours (during business hours)

**Even if just:**
- "Got it, checking on that now"
- "Great question - let me find out"

**Why:**
- Reduces client anxiety
- Shows you're responsive
- Builds trust

### Best Practice 5.2: Use Descriptive Filenames

**Bad:** `scan001.pdf`, `document.pdf`, `contract_final_v3.pdf`

**Good:** `Inspection_Report_123MainSt.pdf`, `Appraisal_456OakAve.pdf`

1. Click document → "Add Note"

3. Save

**Why:** Future you will thank present you

### Best Practice 5.4: Review Document Hub Weekly

**Once a week, spend 5 minutes:**

1. Open each active transaction

5. Delete duplicates

**When:** Friday afternoon (before weekend)

**Why:** Keep things organized, avoid clutter

## Category 8: Avoiding Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Uploading Scanned Handwritten Contracts

**Problem:** AI can't read handwriting

**Solution:**
- Use digital contracts when possible
- If handwritten, enter data manually
- Takes 2-3 minutes vs. AI extraction failure

---

### Mistake 2: Ignoring Yellow/Red Confidence Scores

**Problem:** Assuming AI is always right

**Solution:**
- Always review yellow/red fields
- Edit if needed
- Trust but verify

---

### Mistake 3: Not Customizing Email Templates

**Problem:** Generic-sounding emails

**Solution:**
- Spend 5 minutes customizing templates
- Add your branding and tone
- Makes big difference in client perception

---

### Mistake 4: Letting Documents Pile Up

**Problem:** Disorganized, can't find what you need

**Solution:**
- Upload immediately when received
- Tag and categorize on upload
- Weekly review to stay organized

---

### Mistake 5: Skipping Onboarding

**Problem:** Not using features optimally

**Solution:**
- Complete onboarding checklist
- Watch 3-minute video
- Book onboarding call if confused

**Time invested:** 10 minutes
**Time saved:** Hours per week
