## The 5 Critical Flaws

### 1. We're Solving the Wrong Problem (High Confidence)

**Evidence:**
- No proof this is a TOP priority (just A priority)
- Research came from TC industry (selection bias)
- 0 conversations with actual agents
- Problem might be "annoying" not "painful enough to pay for"

**Impact:** CRITICAL - If agents don't care, nothing else matters

**How to Disprove:**
- 10 agents say "I'd pay for this" unprompted
- 5 agents share stories of losing money from missed deadlines

---

### 2. We're Building the Wrong Solution (High Confidence)

**Evidence:**
- AI adoption for low-stakes tasks ≠ AI adoption for high-stakes
- "Good enough" solutions already exist (human TCs, Dotloop, spreadsheets)
- Control-freak agents may reject automation
- AI might create more work (verification burden)

**Impact:** CRITICAL - Even if problem exists, this solution might fail

**How to Disprove:**
- 5 agents try prototype and say "this saved me time"
- Accuracy >98% with real contracts
- Agents trust it after using it

---

### 3. The Market is Too Small (Medium-High Confidence)

**Evidence:**
- Realistic TAM: 10,000 agents (not 600,000)
- CAC likely >$600, LTV ~$1,800 (risky unit economics)
- High churn industry (10%+ monthly)
- No distribution advantage

**Impact:** HIGH - Can't build a meaningful business even if we execute perfectly

**How to Disprove:**
- NAR data shows 100,000+ teams in target segment
- Find channel with CAC <$300
- Early churn <5% monthly

---

### 4. No Competitive Moat (Very High Confidence)

**Evidence:**
- Dotloop/Skyslope can add AI in 6-12 months
- They have more data, users, money, distribution
- AI extraction is becoming a commodity
- No network effects, switching costs, or proprietary advantages

**Impact:** CRITICAL - We get crushed when incumbents notice us

**How to Disprove:**
- Achieve 99% accuracy (others can't match)
- Get exclusive partnership (they can't access)
- Reach 10,000 users before competitors react (unlikely)

---

### 5. Kill Criteria Will Trigger (High Probability)

**Most likely death scenario:**
1. 50 conversations → 0 "I want this" (40% probability)
2. Incumbent crushes us with free AI features (30% probability)

**Timeline:** Within 4-8 weeks

**Impact:** CERTAIN - One of these will likely happen

## The Path Forward

### Option A: Validate Fast (Recommended)

**Goal:** Prove we're wrong in 4 weeks

**Actions:**
1. **Week 1-2:** Have 30 conversations with agents
   - Ask about top 3 challenges (see if ours comes up)
   - Show prototype, watch reaction
   - Ask "Would you pay $99 for this?"

2. **Week 3-4:** Test acquisition
   - Spend $500 on Facebook ads
   - Measure CAC to get a trial
   - Get 10-20 people to try it

3. **Week 4:** Decision
   - If 5+ say "I want this": proceed
   - If 0 say "I want this": kill or pivot
   - If mixed: iterate or pivot

**Cost:** 4 weeks, $500-1,000
**Risk:** Low (find out fast)
**Reward:** Certainty

## The Final Question

**Are we building a feature or a business?**

**Feature:**
- Solves one specific problem
- Easily copied by incumbents
- No defensible position
- Value: acquihire ($1-5M)

**Business:**
- Solves multiple problems
- Has network effects or moat
- Defensible position
- Value: $50-500M+ exit

**Current state:** We're building a feature.

**Can it become a business?** Only if:
1. We find a moat (unlikely)
2. We pivot to a bigger opportunity (possible)
3. We accept it's a feature and aim for acquihire (honest)
