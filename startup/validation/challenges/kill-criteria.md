# Kill Criteria: 5 Scenarios That Would End This Business

**Date:** 2026-03-17
**Purpose:** Define clear death scenarios so we recognize them early
**Mental Model:** "If X happens, we shut down or pivot immediately"

---

## Kill Criterion #1: The "No One Wants This" Death

### The Scenario
After 50 conversations with real estate agents:
- 0 agents say "I'd pay for this"
- 40+ agents say "I don't have this problem" or "My current system works fine"
- No one gets excited when you describe the solution
- People are being polite, not genuinely interested

### The Evidence
- **Metric:** <5% of conversations result in "I want this"
- **Metric:** 0 agents ask "When can I try it?"
- **Metric:** 0 agents ask about pricing
- **Behavioral:** People don't lean in, they lean back

### Why This Kills Us
If 50 agents don't want it, 50,000 won't either.
The problem isn't "we haven't found the right customers yet."
The problem is "we're solving the wrong problem."

### What To Do
- **Shut down:** Yes, immediately
- **Don't:** Keep building, keep pitching, keep hoping
- **Pivot to:** Different problem in real estate OR different industry entirely

### Timeline to Detect
**4 weeks** (50 conversations = 2-3 per day for 3 weeks)

## Kill Criterion #2: The "Unit Economics Death Spiral"

### The Scenario
After 6 months of trying to acquire customers:
- CAC is $800+ (Facebook ads, cold outreach, partnerships)
- LTV is $1,200 (agents churn within 8 months)
- CAC:LTV ratio is 1:1.5 (need 1:3 minimum)
- Every customer acquired burns cash
- Growth requires external capital, but VCs won't invest

### The Evidence
- **Metric:** CAC > $600 after optimization
- **Metric:** Monthly churn > 8%
- **Metric:** LTV < $1,500
- **Metric:** Can't reach profitability at any scale

### Why This Kills Us
Unit economics don't improve with scale in B2B SaaS.
If CAC > LTV at 10 customers, CAC > LTV at 1,000 customers.
We'd need to 10x LTV or 0.1x CAC - neither is realistic.

### What To Do
- **Shut down:** If CAC can't be reduced AND LTV can't be increased
- **Don't:** Keep burning cash hoping it gets better
- **Pivot to:** Different pricing model (per-transaction?) or different market with better economics

### Timeline to Detect
**6 months** (need enough data to be confident)

## Kill Criterion #3: The "Accuracy Trust Gap" Death

### The Scenario
We build the product, launch it:
- AI accuracy is 90-95% (impressive for AI, unacceptable for legal)
- Users find 5-10% of extractions have errors
- One error causes a missed deadline → lost deposit → angry client
- Word spreads: "Don't trust that AI tool, it screws up contracts"
- Reputation destroyed, can't recover

### The Evidence
- **Metric:** Accuracy <98% after 3 months of improvements
- **Metric:** Users report errors in >5% of transactions
- **Metric:** 1+ public complaint about accuracy causing real damage
- **Behavioral:** Users say "I can't trust it, I check everything anyway"

### Why This Kills Us
Real estate agents are liability-obsessed.
One horror story = 100 lost potential customers.
If AI can't be trusted, it's worthless for this use case.
We can't "fix it later" - trust lost is trust gone forever.

### What To Do
- **Shut down:** If accuracy can't reach 98%+ within 6 months
- **Don't:** Keep selling a broken product
- **Pivot to:** Lower-stakes use of AI (e.g., initial data entry, not final)

### Timeline to Detect
**3-6 months** (need real-world usage data)

## Kill Criterion #4: The "Incumbent Crush" Death

### The Scenario
We get some traction (100+ users, $15K MRR):
- Dotloop/Skyslope announces AI features
- They launch within 3 months
- They give it away for FREE to existing users
- Our users churn back to their existing tools
- We can't compete with free + integrated

### The Evidence
- **Event:** Major competitor launches AI extraction feature
- **Metric:** Churn spikes to 20%+ monthly
- **Metric:** New signups drop 50%+
- **Metric:** Competitor's feature is 80% as good for free

### Why This Kills Us
We can't beat "free + integrated + trusted."
Our entire value prop is commoditized.
No differentiation = no reason to exist.

### What To Do
- **Shut down:** If we can't find a differentiated niche within 2 months
- **Don't:** Try to out-feature them (we can't)
- **Pivot to:** Niche they won't serve (luxury? commercial? specific state?)

### Timeline to Detect
**Day 1 of competitor launch** (immediate impact)

## Kill Criterion #5: The "Market Crash" Death

### The Scenario
Real estate market crashes (rates spike, prices drop, volume collapses):
- Transaction volume drops 40%+ (like 2008, 2020)
- Agents leave the industry in droves
- Remaining agents have 1-2 deals/month (don't need automation)
- Churn spikes as agents cancel all subscriptions
- New signups plummet (no one's hiring tools when they're broke)

### The Evidence
- **Macro:** Interest rates >7%, transaction volume down 30%+
- **Metric:** Churn spikes to 20%+ monthly
- **Metric:** New signups drop 60%+
- **Behavioral:** Agents say "I can't afford any subscriptions right now"

### Why This Kills Us
We're a discretionary expense for agents.
When times are tough, subscriptions get cut first.
Can't survive a 2-3 year downturn without massive cash reserves.

### What To Do
- **Shut down:** If downturn lasts >12 months with no recovery
- **Don't:** Burn cash hoping for recovery
- **Pivot to:** Tool for DOWN markets (e.g., help agents survive, not thrive)

### Timeline to Detect
**1 month** (immediate impact when market turns)

## The "Soft Kill" Scenarios

These won't kill us immediately, but they'll make us wish we were dead:

### Soft Kill #1: The "Stuck at $5K MRR" Plateau
- Can't grow past $5K MRR for 12+ months
- Market too small, or we can't reach more customers
- **Verdict:** Shut down - not worth the opportunity cost

### Soft Kill #2: The "Founder Burnout" Death
- Working 60+ hours/week for 12+ months
- Revenue growing but not enough to hire help
- No end in sight, no life outside work
- **Verdict:** Shut down - mental health > startup

### Soft Kill #3: The "Acquihire Temptation"
- Offer to be acquired for $500K-1M (acquihire price)
- Better than shutting down, but not a real success
- **Verdict:** Take it if offered, but admit this isn't what we wanted

## The Decision Framework

### When to Quit (Kill Criteria Met):

**Any ONE of these:**
- 50 conversations, 0 "I want this"
- CAC >$800, LTV <$1,200, no path to improvement
- Accuracy <95% after 6 months
- Competitor launches free version, churn >15%
- Market crashes, revenue drops 50%+

**Action:** Shut down within 30 days

### When to Pivot:

**Any ONE of these:**
- Problem validated but solution rejected
- Solution validated but market too small
- Unit economics bad but fixable with different model
- One segment loves it, everyone else hates it

**Action:** Pivot within 60 days

### When to Persevere:

**ALL of these:**
- 10+ agents say "I'd pay for this"
- CAC <$400, LTV >$1,800
- Accuracy >98%
- No major competitive threat
- Growing >10% MoM

**Action:** Keep going, raise capital if needed
