# Solution Challenge: Is AI Extraction Actually Wanted?

**Date:** 2026-03-17
**Challenge Level:** CRITICAL
**Confidence in Solution Being Wanted:** 15% (down from 20%)

---

## The Core Assumption

**We believe:** AI that extracts contract data and auto-generates timelines will be valuable.

**But what if we're solving a problem agents don't have?**

---

## Challenge #1: Do Agents WANT Automation?

### Evidence FOR Automation:
- 82% use AI for descriptions
- 74% use AI for marketing
- 75%+ firms increasing AI investment

### What This Actually Means:
- They use AI for **low-stakes tasks** (descriptions, marketing)
- These tasks have **no legal liability**
- Errors are **embarrassing, not expensive**

### What We're Asking:
- Let AI read **legal contracts**
- Trust AI with **deadline calculations**
- Accept **liability for errors**

**This is a TOTALLY different risk profile.**

---

## Challenge #2: The "Good Enough" Problem

### Existing Solutions:

**Human TCs ($3-5K/month):**
- 100% accurate (when good)
- Handle edge cases
- Provide accountability
- Build relationships
- **Why switch?** Cost? But if you can afford $3-5K, cost isn't the problem

**Existing Software (Dotloop, Skyslope, Follow Up Boss):**
- Already used
- Already paid for
- Already integrated
- Team already trained
- **Why switch?** AI features? They can add those

**DIY Solutions:**
- Google Calendar + spreadsheets (free)
- Works for low volume
- "I can handle it" mentality
- **Why pay $99-199 when free works?**

---

## Challenge #3: The AI Trust Gap

### High-Stakes vs. Low-Stakes AI

| Task | Stakes | Error Cost | Trust Needed | Current Adoption |
|------|--------|------------|--------------|------------------|
| Write description | Low | Embarrassment | Low | HIGH (82%) |
| Create marketing | Low | Waste time | Low | HIGH (74%) |
| Extract contract data | HIGH | Lawsuit, lost deal | HIGH | UNKNOWN |
| Calculate deadlines | HIGH | Lost deposit | HIGH | UNKNOWN |
| Send reminders | MEDIUM | Annoyance | Medium | MEDIUM |

**The Pattern:** Agents use AI for tasks where errors don't cost money.

**Our Solution:** Use AI for tasks where errors DO cost money.

**See the problem?**

---

## Challenge #4: What If AI Creates MORE Work?

### The "AI Burden" Hypothesis

**Scenario:**
1. AI extracts data from contract
2. Agent must verify every extraction (because errors)
3. Agent must double-check every deadline (because liability)
4. Agent must override AI when it's wrong
5. Agent spends MORE time supervising AI than doing it themselves

**Result:** AI adds a step, doesn't remove one.

**This is the "self-driving car" problem:**
- Level 2 automation requires constant supervision
- Often MORE stressful than just driving
- Level 5 (full automation) is what people actually want
- But we're building Level 2

---

## Challenge #5: The Control Freak Factor

### Real Estate Agent Psychology

**Type A Personalities:**
- "I need to control everything"
- "I don't trust anyone"
- "If I want it done right..."
- "My way or the highway"

**These are the SUCCESSFUL agents.**

**Question:** Do control freaks want AI handling their transactions?

**Likely Answer:** They want humans they can supervise, not black-box AI they can't.

---

## Three Scenarios Where Solution Fails

### Scenario A: "I Don't Trust It"
- Agent says: "What if it misses a deadline? I'm liable."
- Agent says: "I've seen AI hallucinate. Not with my contracts."
- Agent says: "I need to check everything anyway, so why bother?"
- **Risk Level:** HIGH

### Scenario B: "My Current System Works"
- Agent has TC: "She knows my clients, handles everything"
- Agent has software: "Dotloop works fine, I'm used to it"
- Agent has spreadsheet: "I have 3 deals a month, I don't need AI"
- **Risk Level:** HIGH

### Scenario C: "AI Makes It Worse"
- Agent tries it: "It extracted wrong dates twice"
- Agent tries it: "I spent 30 minutes correcting it"
- Agent tries it: "Easier to just enter myself"
- **Churn immediately**
- **Risk Level:** MEDIUM-HIGH

---

## What Would Prove Us Wrong

**We're wrong if:**
1. Agents say "I'd never let AI read my contracts"
2. Agents say "I need to control every detail myself"
3. Agents try it and accuracy <95% (unacceptable for legal docs)
4. Agents try it and say "this takes longer"

**We're right if:**
1. Agents say "I hate data entry, please automate it"
2. Agents try it and say "this saved me 2 hours per deal"
3. Agents say "I trust AI more than my assistant"
4. Agents ask "when can I start?"

---

## The Most Dangerous Assumption

**We assume:** "AI adoption for marketing = AI adoption for transactions"

**Reality:**
- Marketing AI: low risk, visible output, easy to verify
- Transaction AI: high risk, invisible processes, hard to verify until it fails
- **Completely different adoption curves**

**Analogous mistake:** 
- "People use calculators, so they'll use AI for taxes"
- No - taxes have AUDIT RISK, calculators don't
- Same logic applies here

---

## The "What If We're Building the Wrong AI?" Problem

### Alternative AI Solutions:

1. **AI that WRITES contracts** (not extracts)
   - Higher value
   - More visible
   - Agents already pay for this ( attorneys)

2. **AI that PREDICTS deal fall-through**
   - Unique value
   - Proactive, not reactive
   - Harder to build, but more valuable

3. **AI that NEGOTIATES**
   - High value
   - Differentiated
   - More exciting than "data extraction"

**Question:** Why are we building the LEAST exciting AI feature?

---

## Challenge Verdict

**Original Confidence:** 20%
**After Challenge:** 15%

**Why:**
- AI trust doesn't transfer from low-stakes to high-stakes
- "Good enough" solutions already exist
- Control freak agents may reject automation
- AI might create more work, not less
- No evidence agents WANT this specific solution

**Critical Next Step:**
Show a prototype to 5 agents. Watch their reaction.
- If they lean in, ask questions, want to try: good sign
- If they shrug, make excuses, change subject: bad sign
- If they say "how accurate is it?": trust is the issue

---

## Red Flag 🚩

We're building "AI for AI's sake" without validating that THIS application of AI is wanted.

82% use AI for descriptions ≠ 82% want AI for contracts

That's like saying "90% use email, so they'll want email for legal notices"
Context matters. Stakes matter. Risk tolerance matters.

**We haven't validated the risk tolerance.**
