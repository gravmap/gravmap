# Conversation Cheat Sheet

Print this out or keep it open during calls.

---

## Before the Call (2 mins prep)
- [ ] Review agent's profile/post
- [ ] Note why you selected them
- [ ] Have conversation template ready
- [ ] Recording/notepad ready

---

## Opening (30 seconds)

> "Thanks for taking the time. I'm building a tool for real estate transaction coordination, but I want to make sure I'm solving real problems—not what I *think* agents need. Can you walk me through your current process for managing transactions?"

**Then SHUT UP and listen.** Take notes.

---

## Core Questions (Use 4-6 of these)

### 1. Pain Discovery
> "What's your biggest pain point with transaction coordination?"

**Listen for:**
- Missed deadlines
- Things falling through cracks
- Communication breakdowns
- Working nights/weekends
- TC issues (if they have one)

### 2. Current Tools
> "What tools do you currently use? Why do you keep using them?"

**Listen for:**
- "We've always used it"
- "It's too complex"
- "I hate it but..."
- "Nothing works well"

### 3. Trust Check
> "Would you trust AI to extract data from contracts? Why/why not?"

**Listen for:**
- Concerns about accuracy
- Need for human review
- Data privacy worries
- Curiosity/openness

### 4. Pricing Validation
> "If a tool could save you 7 hours/week, would you pay $99-199/month?"

**Listen for:**
- "Depends on..." (good - thinking)
- "That seems reasonable" (good)
- "Maybe if it actually works" (good)
- "No way" (red flag)
- "Sure" without thinking (red flag)

### 5. Switching Triggers
> "What would make you switch from your current system?"

**Listen for:**
- Specific pain event (missed deadline)
- TC quitting
- Growth pains
- Nothing (red flag)

### 6. Past Attempts
> "Have you tried automating this before? What happened?"

**Listen for:**
- What failed and why
- Lessons learned
- Never tried (opportunity)

---

## Probing Techniques

**If they say "It's fine":**
> "I hear that a lot. What about when things get busy? Any cracks in the system?"

**If they mention a specific problem:**
> "Tell me more about that. When did that last happen?"

**If they're vague:**
> "If you had to pick one thing that keeps you up at night about transactions, what would it be?"

**If they complain about their TC:**
> "What would the ideal transaction coordinator do differently?"

**If skeptical of AI:**
> "That's a fair concern. What would need to be true for you to feel comfortable?"

---

## Closing (2 mins)

> "This is incredibly helpful. Here's what I'm hearing: [summarize their top 2-3 pain points]. Does that sound right?"

**Wait for confirmation, then:**

> "I'm looking for a small group of agents to test what I'm building. Would you be interested in early access? I'm not selling anything—just want feedback from people who actually deal with this stuff."

**If yes:**
> "Perfect. I'll reach out in the next few weeks when we're ready. In the meantime, I'll send you a summary of what I'm learning from other agents. Thanks again!"

**If maybe:**
> "Totally understand. Mind if I keep you in the loop on what we're building? You can tell me if we're on the right track or totally missing the mark."

**If no:**
> "No worries at all. Thanks for sharing your experience - it's really valuable. I appreciate your time!"

---

## Red Flags to Watch For

🚩 **"Everything works great, no problems"**
- Probe deeper or move on
- May not be target customer

🚩 **"I'd never trust AI for this"**
- Note specific concerns
- May require different positioning

🚩 **"$199 seems expensive"**
- Ask what they currently pay
- Understand budget constraints

🚩 **"I don't have time for this"**
- Thank them and end call
- Not a good fit right now

---

## Green Flags (Good Signs)

✅ **Multiple specific examples of dropped balls**
✅ **Currently paying for TC or expensive tools**
✅ **Working nights/weekends on paperwork**
✅ **Curious about AI despite concerns**
✅ **Asks about pricing without flinching**
✅ **Wants to see the product**
✅ **Offers to introduce other agents**

   - Add to outreach log

## If You Get Stuck

**Running out of questions?**
> "What else should I be asking? What am I missing?"

**They ask about your solution?**
> "I'm still figuring that out, which is why I'm talking to agents like you. What would YOUR ideal solution look like?"

**They want to buy now?**
> "We're not ready yet, but I'd love to keep you in the loop. What's the best email?"

**They're skeptical?**
> "That's fair. I'm not trying to sell you anything today - just learn. What's worked well for you so far?"

---

## Remember

- You're a RESEARCHER, not a SALESPERSON
- LISTEN 80%, talk 20%
- Their REALITY is what matters
- HONEST negative feedback > fake positivity
- Thank them genuinely

**One great conversation beats five rushed ones.**
