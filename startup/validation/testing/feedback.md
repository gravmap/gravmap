# Feedback Collection System

**How to Get Honest Feedback Without Leading Questions**

---

## The Golden Rule of Feedback

**People will lie to be polite.**

They'll say "this is great!" to avoid hurting your feelings.
They'll say "I'd definitely use this!" to avoid awkwardness.
They'll say "I'd pay $99!" when they'd never actually pull out their credit card.

**Your job: Get the truth, not validation.**

---

## Interview Scripts

### Post-Trial Interview (15-20 minutes)

**Purpose:** Understand if they got value from the trial.

**Opening (Neutral, not leading):**
```
"Thanks for trying [Product]. I want to understand your honest 
experience - the good, the bad, and the ugly. No need to be polite, 
I need real feedback to improve."
```

**Usage Questions (Behavior-based):**

1. "Walk me through the last time you used the product. What were you trying to do?"
   - ✅ Gets specific behavior
   - ❌ "How did you like it?" (too vague)

2. "What did you do first when you logged in?"
   - ✅ Reveals natural behavior
   - ❌ "Did you find it easy to navigate?" (leading)

3. "Did you upload a real contract or a test one?"
   - ✅ Reveals trust level
   - ❌ "Did you trust the system?" (abstract)

**Value Questions (Outcome-based):**

4. "What's one thing you were able to do with the product that you couldn't do before?"
   - ✅ Measures real value
   - ❌ "Was this valuable?" (yes-bias)

5. "If you had to explain this product to a colleague, what would you say it does?"
   - ✅ Reveals comprehension
   - ❌ "Do you understand what this does?" (yes-bias)

6. "What would you miss most if this product disappeared tomorrow?"
   - ✅ Reveals stickiness
   - ❌ "Would you miss this?" (yes-bias)

**Pain Points (Problem-based):**

7. "What was the most frustrating part of using the product?"
   - ✅ Invites honest criticism
   - ❌ "Were there any issues?" (too gentle)

8. "Was there a moment where you thought 'this doesn't work for me'?"
   - ✅ Identifies blockers
   - ❌ "Did everything work okay?" (yes-bias)

9. "What would need to change for this to become part of your daily workflow?"
   - ✅ Actionable feedback
   - ❌ "Would you use this daily?" (yes-bias)

**Pricing Questions (Commitment-based):**

10. "The product costs $99/month. How does that feel to you?"
    - ✅ Open reaction
    - ❌ "Would you pay $99/month?" (abstract)

11. "If I asked for your credit card right now, what would you say?"
    - ✅ Reveals true intent
    - ❌ "Is $99 fair?" (subjective)

12. "What would make you hesitate to pay for this?"
    - ✅ Identifies objections
    - ❌ "Any concerns about pricing?" (too gentle)

**Closing (The PMF Question):**

13. "How would you feel if you could no longer use [Product]? Very disappointed, somewhat disappointed, or not disappointed?"
    - ✅ The Sean Ellis test
    - ❌ "Would you be disappointed?" (leading)

### Churn Interview (15-20 minutes)

**Purpose:** Understand why they left (real reasons, not polite excuses).

**Opening (Non-judgmental):**
```
"I noticed you stopped using [Product]. I'm not trying to win you 
back - I just want to understand what happened so I can improve. 
Your honest feedback would be really valuable."
```

**Timing Questions:**

1. "When did you first start thinking about canceling?"
   - ✅ Identifies tipping point
   - ❌ "Why did you cancel?" (too direct, invites excuses)

2. "What was happening in your business around that time?"
   - ✅ Contextualizes decision
   - ❌ "Was it the right time?" (irrelevant)

**Trigger Questions:**

3. "Walk me through the exact moment you decided to cancel. What pushed you over the edge?"
   - ✅ Gets to real trigger
   - ❌ "What made you cancel?" (invites prepared answer)

4. "Was there one specific thing, or was it a bunch of things adding up?"
   - ✅ Distinguishes acute vs chronic
   - ❌ "What was the main reason?" (assumes single reason)

**Alternative Questions:**

5. "What are you using now instead?"
   - ✅ Reveals true competition
   - ❌ "Did you find a better solution?" (binary)

6. "What does your new solution do that we didn't?"
   - ✅ Identifies gaps
   - ❌ "What were we missing?" (defensive framing)

7. "If we could change one thing, would you consider coming back?"
   - ✅ Identifies fixable issue
   - ❌ "What would make you come back?" (implies commitment)

**Honesty Check:**

8. "I know people sometimes say 'pricing' when it's really something else. Can you be honest - was it the price, or something else?"
   - ✅ Cuts through polite excuses
   - ❌ "Was it the price?" (yes/no)

9. "Was there anything about the product that frustrated you but you didn't mention?"
   - ✅ Surfaces unspoken issues
   - ❌ "Any frustrations?" (too generic)

10. "If a friend asked you about [Product], what would you tell them?"
    - ✅ Reveals true sentiment
    - ❌ "Would you recommend us?" (yes-bias)

---

### Sales Objection Interview (10-15 minutes)

**Purpose:** Understand why prospects don't convert.

**Opening:**
```
"I noticed you signed up for a trial but didn't convert to a paid 
plan. I'm not trying to sell you - just want to understand what 
held you back. Your feedback will help me improve."
```

**Objection Questions:**

1. "What was your first reaction when you saw the pricing?"
   - ✅ Emotional response
   - ❌ "Was the pricing too high?" (leading)

2. "What were you hoping to get from the product?"
   - ✅ Reveals expectations
   - ❌ "Did the product meet your expectations?" (yes-bias)

3. "Did anything about the product surprise you - in a good or bad way?"
   - ✅ Open-ended
   - ❌ "What didn't you like?" (negative framing)

4. "What would need to be true for you to become a paying customer?"
   - ✅ Actionable
   - ❌ "What would make you buy?" (assumes they would)

5. "Are you using something else to solve this problem? What is it?"
   - ✅ Competition
   - ❌ "Did you find a better alternative?" (binary)

## NPS Survey Questions

### Standard NPS (Post-Trial)

**The Question:**
```
On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend [Product] 
to a friend or colleague?
```

**Follow-up (Critical):**

For Promoters (9-10):
```
"What's the main reason you gave us a [X]?"
"What one thing could we do to make you even happier?"
```

For Passives (7-8):
```
"What would need to change for you to give us a 9 or 10?"
"What's holding you back from recommending us?"
```

For Detractors (0-6):
```
"I'm sorry we didn't meet your expectations. What went wrong?"
"What's the one thing we could do to improve your experience?"
```

### Transaction-Specific NPS

After each key action, send micro-surveys:

**Post-Upload:**
```
How easy was it to upload your contract?
😊 Very easy | 😐 Neutral | 😞 Difficult
```

**Post-Extraction:**
```
How accurate was the AI extraction?
🎉 Perfect | 👍 Mostly accurate | 👎 Not accurate
```

**Post-Timeline:**
```
How useful is the auto-generated timeline?
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (1-5 scale)
```

```markdown

   >

   >
```

## How to Avoid Leading Questions

### The Trap of Leading Questions

**Leading Questions (AVOID):**
- "Did you find the product easy to use?" → "Yes" (politeness bias)
- "Was this feature helpful?" → "Yeah, I guess" (agreement bias)
- "Would you pay $99 for this?" → "Maybe" (hypothetical)
- "Do you think this is a good idea?" → "Sure" (social pressure)
- "Don't you think X is annoying?" → "Yeah" (confirmation bias)

**Better Questions (USE):**
- "Walk me through how you used the product" → Real behavior
- "What did you do with this feature?" → Actual usage
- "The price is $99. How does that feel?" → Real reaction
- "What's your honest opinion?" → Open-ended
- "What frustrated you?" → Invites criticism

### The Contrast Technique

**Instead of asking what they like, ask what they DON'T like:**

❌ "What features did you like?"
✅ "What features did you NOT use or find confusing?"

❌ "What worked well?"
✅ "What didn't work as expected?"

❌ "What would you keep?"
✅ "What would you remove if you could?"

### The Specificity Test

**Vague questions get vague answers:**

❌ "Did you find it valuable?"
✅ "What specifically did you accomplish using the product?"

❌ "Was it easy to use?"
✅ "How many clicks did it take to [do specific task]?"

❌ "Would you use it again?"
✅ "When's the next time you'd need to use this?"

### The Behavior Test

**Don't ask about opinions - ask about actions:**

| Opinion Question (Weak) | Behavior Question (Strong) |
|------------------------|---------------------------|
| "Would you pay for this?" | "Can I get your credit card now?" |
| "Would you use this daily?" | "How many times did you log in this week?" |
| "Would you recommend this?" | "Have you told anyone about this?" |
| "Is this valuable?" | "What did you actually accomplish?" |
| "Do you like this feature?" | "Did you use this feature?" |

## Feedback Collection Methods

### 1. Live Interviews (Best for depth)

**When:** After trial, after churn, for feature requests
**Format:** Video call (Zoom, Google Meet)
**Duration:** 15-20 minutes
**Pros:** Body language, follow-up questions, depth
**Cons:** Time-intensive, scheduling friction

### 2. In-App Surveys (Best for volume)

**When:** Post-action micro-surveys
**Format:** Popup in product (Hotjar, UserLeap, Typeform)
**Duration:** 1-2 questions max
**Pros:** Immediate, contextual, high response rate
**Cons:** Shallow, easy to dismiss

### 3. Email Surveys (Best for NPS)

**When:** Post-trial, monthly check-in
**Format:** Email with Typeform/SurveyMonkey link
**Duration:** 3-5 questions
**Pros:** Thoughtful responses, convenient
**Cons:** Low response rate, delayed

### 4. In-App Chat (Best for quick questions)

**When:** During usage, for confusion points
**Format:** Intercom, Crisp, Drift
**Duration:** Real-time conversation
**Pros:** Immediate, contextual, low friction
**Cons:** Shallow, interruptive

### 5. Usability Tests (Best for UX)

**When:** Before launch, during onboarding redesign
**Format:** Screen recording + think-aloud
**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Pros:** See real behavior, uncover friction
**Cons:** Time-intensive, requires recruitment

## The Feedback Funnel

### Collect → Categorize → Quantify → Prioritize

**Step 1: Collect All Feedback**
- Every interview recorded
- Every survey response saved
- Every chat log captured
- Every email stored

**Step 2: Categorize by Type**
- Bug reports
- Feature requests
- UX issues
- Pricing objections
- Competition mentions
- Positive signals

**Step 3: Quantify Patterns**
- How many mentioned X?
- How severe is X (1-10)?
- Is this blocking adoption?

**Step 4: Prioritize Action**
- P0: Blocking issues (fix immediately)
- P1: High impact, high frequency (fix soon)
- P2: Medium impact (plan for later)
- P3: Nice to have (someday)

## The "Mom Test" Principles

From Rob Fitzpatrick's "The Mom Test":

1. **Talk about their life, not your idea**
   - ❌ "Would you use a product that does X?"
   - ✅ "Tell me about the last time you [had problem]"

2. **Ask about specifics in the past, not generalities in the future**
   - ❌ "Would you ever use X?"
   - ✅ "When's the last time you did X?"

3. **Talk less, listen more**
   - ❌ 80% talking, 20% listening
   - ✅ 20% talking, 80% listening

4. **Never ask "Would you...?"**
   - ❌ "Would you pay $99?"
   - ✅ "What did you pay to solve this last year?"

5. **Seek commitment, not compliments**
   - ❌ "Do you like my idea?"
   - ✅ "Can I follow up next week to show you the prototype?"

## Red Flags in Feedback

### When Feedback Isn't Honest

**Politeness Indicators:**
- "It's really interesting..." (not valuable)
- "I can see how this would help some people..." (not them)
- "The design is nice..." (superficial)
- "Good luck with this!" (dismissive)

**Real Interest Indicators:**
- "When can I start using this?" (urgency)
- "How do I sign up right now?" (action)
- "Can I pay you today?" (commitment)
- "I already told my colleague about this" (referral)

### When to Discount Feedback

**Discount feedback from:**
- Friends and family (too polite)
- Investors (have agenda)
- Competitors (biased)
- People who've never paid for software (wrong segment)
- People outside your target market (not relevant)

**Weight feedback from:**
- Paying customers (proven value)
- People who've churned (honest about why)
- People in target market (relevant)
- People who've tried alternatives (informed)

## Feedback Review Cadence

### Daily
- Check in-app chat for urgent issues
- Review any survey responses

### Weekly
- Summarize 5-10 key feedback points
- Identify patterns
- Update feature priority list

### Monthly
- Review all feedback from the month
- Calculate NPS and "very disappointed" score
- Identify top 3 issues to fix
- Identify top 3 opportunities to pursue

---

## Summary: The Honest Feedback Checklist

Before considering feedback "valid," ask:

- [ ] Is this from a real user, not a friend?
- [ ] Did they actually use the product, or just see it?
- [ ] Are they in our target market?
- [ ] Did I ask behavior questions, not opinion questions?
- [ ] Did I avoid leading questions?
- [ ] Did I document verbatim quotes?
- [ ] Did I observe their actual behavior?
- [ ] Are they putting skin in the game (time/money/reputation)?

**Honest feedback > Polite feedback. Always.**
